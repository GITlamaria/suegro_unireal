In the Eye of the Mind's Storm

Have you ever woken up before your body has abandoned sleep? You lie in a half-paralysed state yet the mind is crystal clear. Every day worries will not disturb your inner peace, as solutions effortlessly appear to dissolve previously insoluble problems. Your mind's eye, as if no longer confined to your body, is but a point in its own space languishing in a time stood still yet ballooned beyond the present. Then the first stirring of morning brings back another reality, the bustle of daily life with which you feel strangely at odds. In that transition you know that while you are whole, the world beyond is but a smoke screen covering disjointed beliefs and institutions. Soon a compulsion to take up your regular routine and responsibilities takes over. Yet a residual feeling remains that the world is not in harmony with … they way things should be, if only you could figure out how: science and religion are in disagreement over the most fundamental issues of life, more often than not politics and the economy pursue cross purposes, and society's bigotry strains your personal relationships. Is such discord not a sign that all these separate realities are incomplete, possibly flawed and but part of a greater reality? And that our further progress may very well depend on our resolve to restore this dissonance to harmony.

Wide ranging imaginative thoughts and inexplicable experiences have long raised suspicion that indeed there is more to this world of ours than meets the eye. However, speculation, faith and non-repeatable phenomena provide no proof or explanation of a metaphysical world. Yet if a transcending reality does exist then it will also offer a way to substantiate it and, provided our science is not too far off the mark, it will be possible with but minor revisions to describe it scientifically. The presentation at this web site introduces just such a scientific account of unifying some of these separate realities into a universal greater reality. Its texts substantiate that indeed there are other dimensions than the usual ones in which we spend our lives and its main section provides the mathematical relations between our familiar world and these newly recognised dimensions.

Our awareness does not reach beyond the three spatial dimensions we normally experience, because any extra-dimensional perception does not appear to extend survival in our usual environment. Hence, we lack the innate ability for regular perception of such other unseen dimensions that, nevertheless, as related elsewhere at this site are still just space and time. The questions that this revelation raises are many, such as whether these other dimensions will make it possible to cross the vast distances of space within a fraction of a lifetime (defying the speed of light) if not travel through time (of course we already travel through time, however, when we think of time travel we mean a controlled leap to the past or future). Even more profound, will we have an existence in these other dimensions separate from the one in this physical world and so survive the death of our body? In fact this web site introduces such a reality in which we are both observers and actors whose role while coincident during our presence in this world is otherwise detached from its mundane concerns and physical manifestation. Accordingly, the answer to the above and many related questions is that all these expectations may come true, except that they will be realised in this larger universal reality that cannot be readily understood in terms of the very limited sense of our present fractured realities.

The Triumph of Time...

Space and time are the two most basic measures by which we define our situation with respect to our surroundings. We go about our daily lives aware that we can move about in an east-west and a north-south direction as well to some extent up and down: in other words in three spatial directions. However, this spatial environment evolves continually, in some ways so subtly that we are not really aware of its alteration such as that caused by the slow erosion of a mountaintop. Other changes, while not always apparent on a daily basis, can only be appreciated over the course of several seasons, for instance the planting of seedlings and the subsequent crop harvest. Nevertheless, most often we are reminded of the relentless imposition of changes on our surroundings by mankind and by the interactions and changes that we personally initiate: the progress of time. So the passing of time means changes to our spatial environment. Evidently space and time have an intimate relationship with each other even if in our every day life we tend to forget that there is such a connection. Yet hard evidence of its precise nature had eluded science until Albert Einstein devised his theory of relativistic time and space. 'Relativity' showed that space and time somehow become ever more alike, as if they were two different sides of the same coin, the higher the velocities involved and identical when the speed of light is reached, although the theory could not fully explain the nature of this identity. Although this dual nature of 'space-time' is not apparent at the low velocities that we experience in everyday life, experiments with high velocity sub-atomic particles have upheld the theory of Special Relativity. A small aberration in the orbit of the planet Mercury provided an indirect confirmation that General Relativity was also correct http://www.humboldt1.com/~gralsto/einstein/relativ.html.

Not surprisingly the relationship between space and time was found to be unlike that between two dimensions of space. For instance, in our every day world spatial relationships frequently concern distance and direction to some destination, often with a choice of different routes. If our destination were in say in a north-westerly direction, we could choose a route that goes in that general direction and even if it heads a bit too far south we would drive the last part of the trip due north until we had reached our goal. On the other hand had we initially taken an even more southerly route, then the last stretch north toward our destination would simply be longer. However, the direct distance 'as the crow flies' remains unaffected regardless which route we would have chosen: distance is invariable regardless the length of the route we take. In a sense the direct distance resembles what Relativity calls 'interval' except that the latter is calculated from time and space measurements as opposed to the former which may be calculated from the westerly and northerly route segments.

According to Relativity when we measure the time lapse and distance between two events- for instance the appearance and end of a 'falling star' that has entered the earth's atmosphere- we obtain different results on earth compared to those reported by an astronaut aboard a spaceship moving at a great speed with respect to us. It is as if the measurements taken by the astronaut concerned a different route through space-time from the one we experienced, yet each set of measurements will let us calculate the same result for the interval that characterises the falling star.

Specifically, when space and time are accurately measured in a moving environment the results will differ as if it had taken more time for a shorter distance compared to corresponding measurements obtained while stationary. In fact when the event involves velocities that approach or equal the speed of light, this kind of foreshortening effect and slowing down of time will be so pronounced that the spatial part will all but vanish while the advance of time will seem to have come to a standstill. It is the latter effect that is the source of many apparent paradoxes of which possibly the best known has space travellers visit another world, a voyage of a few years away, but upon their return find that meanwhile on earth everyone has aged decades. We will return to Relativity's mysterious interval, however, let us first visit some other worlds of our own to probe the intimate relationship between space and time. Instead of a rocket we will employ some more of our mind's imaginative powers for lift off.

...And Truth

In spite of its success at the scale of cosmic phenomena, no one was able to figure out how Relativity could be applied to the very small scale of the elementary particles that appear to make up the matter of our bodies and the objects we see around us. The presentation in this web site confirms that indeed time is just a different way of looking at space and explains how by adopting the proper viewpoint we can observe elementary particles in such a way that they will be consistent with the space and time of Relativity. You might wonder how this might be accomplished, have we not explored all possible spatial points of view? The answer may be surprising at first but upon reflection will have a lot more common sense then the conventional and Relativistic notion of time: if time only seems different from space because we the observer have assumed an orientation that will not let us see it as space, then time like space must also have three dimensions.

How can we acquire a sense of how it is possible that space can give us an experience of time that is totally different from that of the usual view of our spatial surroundings? Actually a simple demonstration will provide such a foretaste: take any object, say a pen, hold it with its length in the line-of-sight and look at it with only one eye. Notice that the length of the pen is actually invisible to the eye: we only see its tip or whatever faces us and we are unable to see its third dimension - the only reason we can see its length is because we normally have both eyes open so that at least one eye will see another side of the object. This simple example shows that space in a direction away from us (in the line-of-sight) is somehow experienced differently from that facing us. In fact, the closest analogy to the passage of time is the way anything in the line-of-sight intersects our vision. For instance, a stack of photographs with subtle changes in successive scenes will give us the illusion of motion (a phenomenon that motion pictures cleverly exploit), whose speed, i.e. progress in time, we can control by the rate at which each photographic frame is brought into view.

Revolution

Let us examine two more examples, the first introducing the second, to further illustrate how space disguises itself as time. Imagine yourself astride a wooden horse on a carrousel: a merry-go-round at a fair or midway. The carrousel starts to turn; soon it gyrates like an enormous spinning top, still the other horses, their riders and the attendant who walks amongst you remain part of its same merry surroundings that you entered when the attraction was halted between rides. However, during the ride bystanders and the rest of the fair soon whiz by in a blur. Now if the carrousel should be open at the top, on a starry night you might see some star straight above all but unaffected by your rotation. In contrast other stars are moving in a circle at an apparent increased speed the further off-centre they are, with the ones just over the horizon almost as much a blur as the onlookers near the carrousel. Now recall how spinning tops stay upright even after hitting other objects (for instance other spinning tops) until they have all but stopped turning? What keeps them from falling over is due to the so-called gyroscopic action of the spin. Similarly, all revolving masses such as our carrousel will resist a change of the direction of their axis of rotation. Accordingly, it would take force to tilt a turning carrousel; yet any displacement up and down in the direction of the spin axis would not be resisted at all (all it would take is lifting or lowering its weight). Now the interesting thing about spin is that while its axis resists directional change by an external force, it nevertheless can, all by it self, make a kind spiralling motion, the same way spinning tops wobble. Suppose its operator loses control and the runaway speed of the carrousel subsequently causes its bearings to fail catastrophically. Say the carrousel breaks free such that it flies off with its axis twirling like a baton (but horizontally more like a spinning top that was knocked over). Overcoming this frightful experience but still clinging to your horse, you would find yourself cut-off from your familiar world on the outside, now but a confusing blur, at best you might make out the fair's lights flickering on-off as if alternating between daylight and the darkness of night. Thus your small rotating island has become a new reality in which you can orientate yourself while the space of the fair on the outside makes no longer any sense. Likewise, to the onlookers the twirling carrousel no longer has the shape of a revolving disk but has become a blurred sphere, a featureless ball.

As the carrousel's speed increased you would have felt a growing force pull you away from the carrousel's centre toward its edge. However, once the carrousel and you are twirling through the air your horse rips off its anchors and ends up wedged sideways between two poles that were mounted along the edge to hold up the carrousel's canopy. To your surprise you can get off the horse and walk around its base as if it magically possessed gravity. In fact if the onlookers outside would have trained their movie camera on this whole mishap and later play the event back in slow-motion they would see that you had walked everywhere on the inside of the blurred sphere they had seen, with your head toward its centre. They would also have seen that a drink that you spilled had formed a thin film of liquid all over the inside of that illusory sphere. After watching the same amateur movie (following recovery from your ordeal), you might come to the realisation that given enough rotational speed, your body- if it hadn't been for the skin and bones that keep us together- and similarly the horse, in fact the carrousel and all composite matter in it, would like your drink have been drawn in all directions crushed against the inside of the apparent sphere. However, these tugs on the body and other composite matter are opposed by the binding force of its constituents, thus you experienced only a resultant force that kept your feet firmly planted on the inside of that phantom sphere.

While now you may have some notion how space can be made to appear different in all three directions, we have not yet made it sufficiently different, in spite of several farfetched concessions to physical impositions and limitations, to make it appear as time. To get yet closer to our aim we will next take a ride on a much-improved merry-go-round: the space shuttle.

A Magic Carpet Ride

Inside the space shuttle once it has attained orbit, the grasp of earth's gravity largely cancels the very forces that tugged at you and kept you pinned to the inside of the illusive sphere of the twirling carrousel. More so these forces are perfectly cancelled for the centre of our own minuscule gravity- let's call it our mind's eye and rely on our skin and bones to keep the rest of us together. This mind's eye like the intersection of cross-hairs in our camera's viewfinder has no volume: it is an infinitely small point in space. A point that just floats in space without direction or acceleration, unaware of the speed that observers on the earth's surface confer on it. Of course ground control would say that the shuttle and our mind's eye in it are in a circular orbit around the earth. Yet neither speed nor the curved path through space are directly measurable by us and we could just as well dismiss the controllers' insistence that we are moving on the basis of the observable fact that they are the ones who are rotating on a sphere at some distance from us- in fact aren't they, not us, the ones who are accelerated (by gravity). To further our argument we could point out that the same would still be true had mission control sent us in what it would have called the opposite direction- in fact we could be going in any direction and with an increased or decreased ground speed at any lower or higher altitude and it still be all the same to us. And why not make the orbit that the controllers on earth tell us about oval - if only earth would not be in the way we could make the orbit increasingly more elliptical and still not experience any movement. In fact if we would make the furthest point of our ellipse so far that the earth appears as if almost an infinitely small dot in space, we would find ourselves bobbing back-and-forth along (almost) the same line or along however many such lines of a different direction that we would care to choose.

Now the point of all this is to demonstrate that our orbits could cover all of space and yet it would be all the same point to our mind's eye. The reason is that our mind's eye experiences no acceleration that would indicate it is starting to move somewhere: it considers itself at rest- it does not make any progress in its space no matter how long- indefinitely and, long after our skin and bones have ceased to exist, to eternity. Thus we could imagine any set of intersecting orbits that are populated by a section of our mind's eye, yet since none of these ever moves anywhere they would admit that at times in their eternal persistence they experience a coincidence: a coincidence not in space but in time only. In other words all space occupies just one infinitely small point for all time: our mind's eye has achieved a position from which the entire universe is observed as- wait …- since there is no other space- as time, going in all directions: time has the same three dimensions as space, and it not only appears likewise but is no different from space for only the viewpoint of our observation has changed. Of course, while time may look just like space, the things in it such as the moon and stars would come to resemble the occasional blurs that we remember from the disastrous carrousel ride. Incredibly we have changed our viewpoint from the closed sphere of all possible shuttle orbits that the ground controllers might observe, to one in which we are the centre of the most complex motions that everything else seems to undergo. A feat as if we had managed to turn an onion the size of the universe inside-out. An inverted onion whose former centre has become the outermost layer, while its skin is now nothing more than part of that infinitesimal point that we let our mind's eye occupy, all of space in our everyday universe compressed into a single point for all time.

Down to Earth

So now we recognise a situation in which in a blink of an eye- that transition between two moments in time- we can not only see all of space but another situation just like it, in which our mind's eye never closes to see all of time, while its space is no more than a point- a single intersection with our usual space. However, we also recognise that neither represents the world in which we live, for that world somehow has a bit of both. We see two space dimensions directly and our eyes and brain together figure out a third: depth in the line-of-sight. Thus on the one hand, part of us acknowledges the three dimensions of space at any moment in time, while on the other hand we only sense two space dimension directly with the third inferred in the line-of-sight, giving us the experience of another dimension: not all but one arrow of time. Evidently one way or another, the mind's eye's physical embodiment is also its limitation that allows us to experience no more than three dimensions.

Sinusstructor

The act of observing an event involves space-time measurements in a known setting- a frame of reference. Each measurement provides direction and a measure for how far any event (i.e. a point on an Einstein interval hyperbola) is from some agreed reference direction and common point of origin. In mathematics the combination of direction and magnitude (a measure of some quantity like length) are represented by what is called a vector, in effect an arrow of a certain length and direction. Like direction signs on a traffic pole, vectors can have a point in common and provided they point in different directions in effect define the angle between the surfaces on which they lie. Hence, a vector can also be the line in which two surfaces intersect each other (i.e. like the traffic pole to which the direction signs are connected and where they in effect meet). It turns out that there is a way of connecting the vectors representing the measurements of space and time mentioned earlier, including the latter's newly identified dimensions, in a diagram. The symmetrical geometric figure that results has eight surfaces- a so-called octahedron, and is shown in the home page of this web site. What makes the octahedron so interesting is that it derives directly from the mathematical equations of Relativity (those defining the Einstein interval and its so-called Lorentz transformations). Its derivation and the fact that it can be constructed at all are the result of explanations, presented at this web site, why attempts to apply Relativity to the elementary objects that make up the material world around us failed. A simple oversight became a formidable barrier to finding the philosophical basis for measuring the space and time components of elementary particles (called quanta) in such a way that they be consistent with Relativity.

The cause for the mental block that has stymied efforts to advance Physics has no easy parallel in our macroscopic world. Nevertheless, some 'alternate reality' video games may provide some sense of the trap that science fell into. These games provide a visually simulated fantasy world with which the player interacts through the monitor and manual controls, e.g. the point-and-click of a computer mouse. However, no other physical stimulation or queues such as movement, temperature etc. are provided to enforce the player's sense of immersion into the alternate reality of the game. With the benefit of the player's knowledge of the real world on which the fantasy world is loosely modelled, the mind's imagination compensates for the missing sensations. Imagine what would happen if one were brought into the world the child of deranged psychiatrists (no insinuation to demerit the field of psychiatry is intended- it just happens to serve the present example). Suppose these delusional parents had decided by way of experiment to raise their child entirely in the fantasy world of such a video game, with as only concession to the real world the provision of nutrients, for excretion and mechanical stimulation for the child's physical development. Lacking any other clues, the child would come to accept the simulated world as its own and rationalise everything in it to be consistent with the limits of its confines. The correlation of hand movements of the mouse with motion on the screen would likely present a conceptual problem not unlike that posed by the Relativity and Quantum theories: the connection between the two would be inaccessible. Obviously, neither the action on the screen nor of the computer mouse can be associated with activities and their effect that the child does not know such as real motion and acceleration when walking or jumping.

It may be appreciated that under these circumstances it would be difficult for the child to devise a consistent model of its world and next to impossible to extrapolate the real world from the sparse queues that happen to parallel the larger world out there. Yet if it should make such an attempt it would almost inevitably use a 'bottom up' approach trying to extend that which it knows from its simulated reality to built connections to our real world. For instance, in order to come up with a more comprehensive explanation for the correlation between clicking the mouse and initiation of some action on the screen it might theorise that the sound from clicking the mouse is the medium for launching a next step in the game. If it were to try figure out the three dimensional rendering on the flat screen of the monitor it would be at an even greater loss and every movement that derives from the three-dimensions of the real world would need a two-dimensional explanation. In addition, two-dimensional objects on the screen possess 'magical' properties that lets your screen presence disappear, where in the real world you merely move through an opening or behind the object. Its theory of moving objects probably would ultimately develop such that it could explain why, while they are 'obviously' flat, movement seems to bring out a certain foreshortening of the most forward surfaces in the direction of motion (as the object moves across the screen these surfaces disappear altogether when they arrive at the middle of the screen and even though the object continues to move the surfaces remains hidden)- reaching this understanding would somewhat parallel that of Albert Einstein in his theory of Relativity.

Not surprisingly the child's attempts to connect its understanding of motion through the scenes on the screen with that of the disappearance phenomenon would be doomed. What it would need to do is take a bold step outside its two dimensional world where it will find another dimension that will let the child go through an opening itself and also let it see these surfaces without foreshortening if viewed in the direction of motion. The child would discover that shrinkage of these surfaces is but a visual effect of what we call 'perspective' and to understand both it and the disappearance phenomenon, it had needed to perceive that third dimension. All at once its various theories of its two-dimensional world would become whole and consistent, … except that … the child would also realise that what might have been an all consuming quest, its theories while sort of interesting are no longer all that important. Never mind that this new world will for instance offer the possibility of abandoning the game and starting a new one, possibly adopting another game character or playing a different game all together. Unfortunately, all the skills it learned in its two-dimensional world will have a wholly different, possibly only marginal value in a three-dimensional reality. Ironically in the process of growing up in its confinement the child had learned other things that it had never given a second thought, yet now these seem as relevant albeit as distant as its formal theories, … because before it … beyond the computer beckons a new much larger world to discover.

The above parable illustrates why science had failed to identify the conjunction between Relativity and Quantum Mechanics, oversights in each theory reinforced each other: without acknowledgement of the dimensionality of time ultimate subjectivity of reality appears inconceivable and vice versa. Once these concepts are accepted the next step follows as a matter of course: an elementary (quantum) object is its own reference frame with innate spatial and time measurements that are separate from (but not unrelated to) those of the observer and its context. Yet ultimately time measurements always relate to the observer's 'universal time' in its reference frame- i.e. a measure of an object's time is not directly accessible to the observer. Accordingly, a spatial measurement obtained from the object will originate in a separate frame from the one in which time is measured. Conversely, a spatial measurement that originates in the same reference frame as time does not concern a spatial characteristic of the object but always pertains to its position in the space of the observer. Hence, an 'interval' that is calculated from these measurements has a physical meaning that can only be related to the observer and not to the innate characteristics of the object. Nevertheless, the separate origins of spatial and time measurements will suffice to probe an object's characteristics. Aided by time's dimensionality which permits an alternate way of interpreting Relativity's equations, the Lorentz transformations will convert a measurement obtained in one frame to the corresponding one in the other frame so that an equivalent set of measurements in the object's frame may be obtained. As a result, not only will an object's characteristics be revealed, but they are also shown to share a larger common 'universal' reference frame with the observer.

For most of the 20th century this mental block sent physicists into different directions where ever more convoluted schemes were uncovered that forced the adoption of obscure mathematical language to describe them. The fact that the mathematical equations that define the octahedron apply to both the space-time geometry of Relativity and the microscopic world of quanta, resolves what had become the most vexing problem in 20th century physics: the unification of all fundamental forces observed in nature. Most importantly, the properties of the vectors that make up the octahedron and their relationships explain two 'hold-outs': electromagnetic radiation and gravity, and shows that they rest on the same fundamental principles. In fact gravity and our infinite shuttle orbits derive from the same geometrical property, while electromagnetic radiation propagates throughout space in the form of so-called sine waves (their spectrum includes light) whose magnitude varies in the same way as our space shuttle when it was bobbing back-and-forth (like a clock's pendulum). Interestingly the observer cannot perceive the octahedron in its entirety: it may observe its wave model or otherwise encounter a discontinuity that provides the basis for the quantum nature of the reality we experience.

The Catalyst

The 'Foreword' in the main text infers that the understanding that underlies this presentation was not the result of the usual 'bottom up' effort but rather that of a 'top-down' delivery. The following explanation is offered as an introduction to the nature and background to the lifting of the oversights and accomplishment of unification of the Relativity and Quantum theories.

The unification of space-time geometry and the quantum world mentioned above and detailed in the main text of this web site is supported by relatively minor but essential extensions to mathematics and physics. Yet these new concepts did not solely come about as a consequence of philosophical deliberation and thought experiments. Unification resulted as a natural byproduct of identifying and deconstructing prevailing misconceptions using kind of a finished model as a guide - not unlike cutting puzzle pieces from the picture on the box cover such that they match the partially assembled fragments of the puzzle on the table. This 'finished model' had 'come into my hands' as a result of an experience that I had in the spring of 1979. At the time I was asked if I would explain some aspect of electromagnetism- I do not recall the actual question. Not long into my reply it occurred to me that its conventional approach seemed more difficult to understand than warranted and likely would lead to only more questions. I had an intuition that a thought experiment involving 'a ride aboard an electrical field' would lead to a clearer more expedient Relativistic explanation. The informality of the situation had suppressed the fact that Relativity offered no such explanation and soon I found myself resorting to a line of reasoning that took me beyond the accepted context of Relativity. It was this point of departure that as if triggered a vision of how physical reality fit together and- in retrospect not surprising as my expanded perception concerned what some had dubbed the 'theory of everything'- engulfed me in an avalanche of its ramifications.

The experience was instantaneous without forewarning and monumental, yet I managed to finish my explanation without indication of what had happened and without attracting queries over my unorthodox treatment of the subject. However, it conferred on me what metaphorically I have likened to a 'picture on the cover of a puzzle box'. I realised that while complete, the picture as the case of a puzzle box cover did not identify the shape of the pieces or the ones missing from the puzzle of reality and its many parts that had already been assembled. More specifically, its depiction of reality did not aim to provide proof but to provide guidance to the task of completing the puzzle. Since such undertaking would be a lengthy affair, I could not let time dull the experience and therefore set out to capture it in words. Having freed myself from the concentration that scientific proofs demand, an impressionistic rendition held the promise of preserving the essence and would let inspiration and faith in the integrity of that inspiration lead the process. The resulting text could then be a proxy for the understanding gained in the experience to serve as a catalyst for reflections on this intractable subject and as a guide to subsequent works with a more focussed objective or scientific purpose. Nevertheless, the 'core' equations in the main text at the 'unireal' web site were already included in the otherwise esoteric text of this 'catalyst' and their texts' parallels are unmistakable.

Whence Will You Go Next?

If you were able to follow the above narrative you may now feel better equipped to tackle the text in the main presentation of this web site. Those unfamiliar with mathematics, particularly trigonometry may need to skip the equations and accept the associated explanations and implications at face value, and generally may find the treatment of this subject too abstract. Even so, there are parts and animated examples in the main text that will be accessible to many; also the links to other subtexts may now be easier to understand, including surprisingly the two on mathematics. Fare ye well.
