WHO IS WORTHY?
  1. Class theory when expanded to account for temporal relations between propositions will extend into a space-time continuum comprising more than one temporal dimension.
  2. Vector theory can be expanded beyond algebraic application of imaginary vectors to a vector space that is the imaginary counterpart to real 3-space: the complex vector space between this imaginary 3-space and real 3-space is hyperbolic; the extended vector space is congruent with the space-time continuum in 1. above.
  3. Lorentz geometry is ambivalent in its treatment of space-time: the relation between a spatial vector and time being less than a scalar- no sign, and less than a vector- one direction (ostensibly orthonormal to real space), disqualifies it as truly geometric. Invariance of the Einstein interval is thus relegated to a mere algebraic relation, i.e. its individual members of the same class are not equal, hence, the equation of intervals is not a true identity: it cannot qualify for 'three bars'. A resolution that is congruent with 2. above removes the inconsistency such that the equation becomes a true identity.
  4. An elementary (quantum) object's intrinsic characteristics can be related to Lorentz geometry: while temporal measurement is made in the observer's unprimed frame, its spatial measurement (unlike its macroscopic observations) obtains from the primed frame of the object. Hence, the Einstein interval is not a relevant parameter of an elementary object, but the vector sum of spatial and temporal measurements 'one with and the other without a prime' is. The resolution in 3. above also permits another observational perspective: that of an elementary object in two Lorentz frames whose difference to the observer is (only) the parallax angle between their respective spatial coordinates.
  5. Lorentz transformations when applied to 4. above yield vector equations that define a real (Euclidean) 3-space and an imaginary 3-space that is congruent with 2. above. Imaginary vectors regardless of their angular relationship in imaginary 3-space will appear to be orthonormal to real 3-space, conform 3. above.
  6. Extended space-time geometry of 5. above is topologically symmetrical, its complex vector spaces intersect in a complex axial vector- the Einstein interval- which explains the latter's invariance , i.e. they are hyperbolic and consistent with Lorentz geometry which becomes a subset of the 'extended 3-space'.
  7. Quantum Mechanics is interpreted such that the reality of an event (while subjective) is deemed at least identical for all observers (the 'Copenhagen' interpretation). This assertion is incompatible with the validity of observer-event interaction: to an observer other observers are simply synonymous with other events, consequently all observers are mutually subjective. Hence, while an observer will receive a consistent account of an event from each of the other observers, individual observers will not necessarily witness the same reality of an event. Reality ultimately and inexorably is fundamentally subjective in Lorentz space-time, yet congruent and thus objective in the 'extended 3-space' in 6. above, i.e. 6. explains 7. which in turn affirms 6.

Since 7. above imposes a firm and possibly unwelcome constraint on 'the scientific method', this account has instead been unveiled at this web site.
